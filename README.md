# CS-250-Module8Journal
Sprint Review and Retrospective deliverable from your final project.

- How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
- How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
- What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

  Interpreting user needs starts with understanding what people are trying to accomplish, not just what they say they want. Writing clear user stories helps translate those needs into actionable requirements by framing each feature from the user’s perspective answering who it’s for, what they need, and why it matters. This keeps development grounded in real goals rather than assumptions. When I approach building programs, I try to break work into small, testable pieces and iterate quickly. Incorporating Agile practices like short sprints, continuous feedback, and regular retrospectives helps me stay flexible and adapt as requirements evolve.

  Being a strong contributor on a development team means communicating clearly, sharing knowledge, and supporting others when challenges come up. Good team members give and receive feedback respectfully, write maintainable code, and stay open to different ideas and approaches. In an Agile environment, collaboration is essential, whether that’s participating actively in stand ups, helping refine backlog items, or pairing with teammates to solve tricky problems. Ultimately, good software development is as much about people and process as it is about code.
